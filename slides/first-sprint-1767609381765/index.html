What Even Is Agile?

* group of software engineers published a manifesto in 2001 - urging for a new way to work on software * was largely a reaction to the waterfall method * agile is a set of principles - it is not a specific methodology * why can't i just use a todo list? * i heard something about waterfalls? * https://www.atlassian.com/agile/project-management/project-management-intro

Waterfall?

* clearly defined steps that are sequential - hard to go back to revisit * works great for things like building a car where once you have customized the factory robots there is no going back and changing the design

    * self assessment on progress
        * how is your progress? 
        * tentative plan for upcoming weeks
          * current week: goals
          * week #: goals
          * week #: goals, etc
    * briefly summarize any other topics/discussions
* is a combo meeting * review, retrospective, and planning meeting all in one! * this is a format for your meeting agenda and notes - all we really care about is to know that you met and discussed the import things. what you worked on, and what you will work on. we've found this format to be good - but if you have a different format please use that. * DO NOT DO THIS AFTER THE MEETING

* i know there's a lot of text in the milestone... but its not rocket science - you just have to maintain a current sprint and issues that are sorted * as the week goes you can add issues into the new issues pile and wait until the retrospective to sort them out * each subtask is one code session or thereabouts * using milestones will give you a way to group your weekly tasks even when the are just parts of epics - remember epics are for long feature - milestones group tasks by week - so you can see the progress you are making
